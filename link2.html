Dubai- A city of Possibilities

Dubai, a vibrant metropolis in the United Arab Emirates (UAE), has emerged from humble beginnings to become a global icon. Here are some intriguing aspects of this sun-soaked city:
Pearl Diving and Trade: Before oil was discovered in the late 1960s, Dubai’s main trade was pearl diving. In the early 20th century, hundreds of dhows (traditional wooden boats) worked from Dubai Creek, employing thousands of men. Ancient History: Archaeological finds, including spearheads, reveal that humans inhabited the area that is now Dubai over 100,000 years ago. The city’s oldest building is Al Fahidi Fort, dating back to 1787. Innovation and Records: Dubai is home to the world’s first fully functional 3D-printed building, known as The Office of the Future. Expo 2020 Dubai, the first World Expo in the Middle East, welcomed 192 nations and 24 million visitors. Aviation Hub: Dubai International Airport (DXB) is the world’s busiest international airport, handling over 90 million passengers annually. Terminal 2 of DXB boasts the largest solar energy system in the Middle East. Guinness World Records: Dubai holds numerous records, including the world’s tallest building (Burj Khalifa), the deepest swimming pool (at Deep Dive Dubai), and the largest landmark sign (in Hatta). Cosmopolitan Melting Pot: Dubai is home to more than 200 nationalities, making it a melting pot of cultures and experiences. Whether by the banks of the Creek or atop Burj Khalifa, Dubai embodies a sense of possibility and innovation. Ultra-Modern Infrastructure: Dubai boasts the Palm Jumeirah, indoor ski slopes, and huge aquariums. Its driverless subway, ultra-fast cars, and scorching summer heat add to its allure. Desert Adventures: Beyond the city, Dubai’s vast desert invites camel rides, dune bashing, and starlit nights. Visitors can experience both the futuristic skyline and the timeless beauty of the desert.








Burj Khalifa

Tallest Building in the World: The Burj Khalifa stands at an astounding height of over 828 meters (2,716.5 feet), making it the tallest building globally. To put it in perspective, it’s three times taller than the Eiffel Tower and twice as high as the Empire State Building in New York City! Freestanding Marvel: Not only is it the tallest building, but the Burj Khalifa also holds the record for being the tallest freestanding structure in the world. Previously, this record was held by the CN Tower in Toronto. 160 Floors and Beyond: With 160 floors, the Burj Khalifa boasts the highest number of stories globally. It’s a vertical city with a total floor area of approximately 310,000 square meters. Residential Heights: Imagine living at a staggering height! The Burj Khalifa houses the highest residential apartment at 385 meters above the ground. Residents enjoy breathtaking views of Dubai’s skyline. Dining in the Sky: For meals with a view, head to At.mosphere, the world’s highest restaurant, located on level 122. Dine while gazing out at the city from a dizzying height of 441 meters. Observation Decks: The Burj Khalifa offers two observation decks: Level 124 and 125: Admire 360° views over Dubai from this spacious viewing deck. Level 148: Access the highest outdoor observation deck in the world. Longest Elevator Travel Distance: The Burj Khalifa’s elevators are no ordinary ones. They hold the record for the longest travel distance in the world. Zoom up to the observation decks in style!
